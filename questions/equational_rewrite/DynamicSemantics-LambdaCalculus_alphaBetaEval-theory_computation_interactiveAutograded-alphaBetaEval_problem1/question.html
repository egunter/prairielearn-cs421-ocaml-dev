Welcome to the Lambda Calculus AlphaBeta Evaluation Tester!

In the following, you will be given a lambda term and you will be asked to incrementally apply the rules of alphaBeta evaluation to full normal form (where no beta reductions are possible, even after using $\alpha$-equivalence).

The lambda ($\lambda$) in lambda terms is represented using the percent symbol (%). Variables must begin with an alphabetic character (upper or lower case), but then may additionally be followed by other alphabetics, numerics (0-9) and prime ('). Any term or subterm may be wrapped in parentheses. Application binds more tightly than abstraction and associates to the left.

Here is a worked example showing the syntax you should use to enter your work. The starting lambda term is is:

(% x. x (% y. x y))((% u. u)(% w. y w))

The steps of one way of working the problem are:

(% x. x (% y. x y))((% u. u)(% w. y w))
-B-> (% x. x (% y. x y)) (% w. y w)
=a= (% x. x (% z. x z)) (% w. y w)
-B-> (% w. y w) (% z. (% w. y w) z)
-B-> y (% z. (% w. y w) z)
-B-> y (% z. y z)

In doing the problem below, every step must be entered separately, pushing the Check button after each individual step.

Each line you enter is of one of two forms:

  • =a= term$\quad$ if the given step is an $\alpha$-equivalence. You may apply $\alpha$-equivalence as any step. There may be instances in which it is necessary. Using $\alpha$-equivalence will not directly gain you points, although doing it incorrectly will count as an error.
  • -B-> term$\quad$ if the given step is a $\beta$-reduction compatible with the definition of $\alpha\beta$-evaluation.
  • Your final term should be one that can not be reduced by $\beta$-reduction any further, even after swapping the term for one that is $\alpha$-equivalent.
You must enter one step at a time and you need to click Check after each step you enter.
